## Writing Strategy

Primary claim:

- A spatially reproducible and clinically associated plasma-secretory bone marrow program in multiple myeloma.

Do not claim:

- Validated clinical biomarker.
- Prospective classifier.
- Treatment-response predictor.
- Completed R-ISS, PFS, or therapy-response validation.
- Direct TXNDC5 validation in GSE299193.

Manuscript writing order:

1. Results-first rewrite: make each subsection match one figure or evidence block.
2. Methods tightening: make score construction, cohort inclusion, and statistical testing reproducible.
3. Background sharpening: define the gap as spatial localization plus cross-cohort clinical association, not plasma-cell biology itself.
4. Discussion discipline: state why the axis is biologically plausible, clinically associated, and still hypothesis-generating.
5. Declarations and cover letter finalization.

## Reviewer Risk Map

Major risk 1:

- Public-data and computational-only design.

Control:

- Frame as integrative transcriptomic evidence, not clinical validation.

Major risk 2:

- Plasma-secretory signal may reflect plasma-cell abundance.

Control:

- Treat this as biological context and limitation; avoid claiming independent tumor-intrinsic mechanism unless supported.

Major risk 3:

- Incomplete R-ISS/PFS/treatment-response validation.

Control:

- Keep these as future MMRF-enabled analyses, not completed results.

Major risk 4:

- GitHub/code package not fully pushed.

Control:

- Complete GitHub push or prepare Zenodo/code archive before upload.
